Lecture 1

What is Film?

Film as Applied Chemistry

  • Light interacting with emulsions cause film to appear
  • Distinctive form of storytelling of the 20th century
  • Profound impact on humanity

Kind of novelty

  • present in penny arcades
  • early films show this origins

Film is a manufactured object

  • one of the first commodities to become mass-produced
  • same principles that went into the production of the automobile went into firm
  • depends on the specialization of labor
    • directors, best boys, producers, etc
  • could do an entire history of film as a manufactured object
  • economically essential to American history, culture, and development

Film is a social form

  • developed into an essential medium of art
  • enhance the human experience
  • less self-consciously artistic movies are often the most socially revealing
  • embeds itself into society
  • vaguely true oversimplication: "democratic art"
  • reached a wider audience than any other form of narrative that had occurred before
    • mostly a visual medium
    • doesn't require any previous ability
    • was a growing medium during a period when the US had a huge immigrant population
  • often more a visual extravaganza than a deep idea
    • appeals across linguistic barriers; the globalized film
    • new development in film history currently
    • ex: Bollywood films are starting to get good at this
  • quickly becomes a profoundly embedded social form
    • for the period when Hollywood studios were operating at full capacity (1930-1955),
    • e vast majority of Americans went to the movies every week
    • routine universal experience

Not going to cover:

  • film as an economic engine
    • how many jobs created
    • directors, actors, theater employees, special effects, etc
    • could make the argument that it's the most productive economic engine developed under capitalism

Early Silent Cinema

Early Examples

- the novelty of motion kept people riveted regardless of content - The Kiss - scandalized by this, banned in many cities - The Electrocution of an Elephant - first snuff film; quite grotesque - idea of a film capturing reality - Fred Ott's sneeze - one of the earliest films ever made - trains coming into stations, Waves on a Shore

Fred Ott Principle

- process that illustrates how film goes from novelty to social form - in under 30 years, we go from Fred Ott sneezing to Charlie Chaplin - this process of development evolves from the discovery of features that are unique or new to the media - the sociological, technological, artistic, and economic changes that allow the development from the Fred Ott film to artistic, advanced films - the early films aren't particularly sophisticated; what matters is the process of evolution in this era - the essential conventions of firm were alien at the birth of film - around a period of 30 years, it goes from a penny arcade entertainment to one of the most expressive art forms available - three phases of media evolution: 1. Imitation 2. Technical Advance 3. Maturity

Phase of Imitation and Patent Warfare

- All kinds of questions up in the air - how long should a film be? - how will it be exhibited? - how is it produced and distributed? - nothing inherent in the technology that causes the system that arises - don't know the peculiarities of the medium yet - imitation of theater - camera still, action in front - theatrical expansive acting - understand film through the metaphor of theater - all of the ancestor systems that lie behind the old technology influence modern film - ex: The Great Train Robbery - early tradition of performance comes from certain theatrical styles of acting - works for theater because the audience is so far away; need to exaggerate - evolves fairly quickly - ex: The Beast At Bay - goes from a sleazy, morally disturbing melodrama to more subtle acting - mythology of rape/violence against women are replicated - gestures more modulated to the movies: can't do that in theater - non-theatrical kind of acting - ex: Musketeers of Pig Alley - first urban crime film - Lillian Gish, a famous film star - performances get so much more restrained

Patent Warfare

- establishment of big studios - patent warfare: distribution methods, how films would be created, how to exploit audiences

Technical Advance

  • once basic industrial stability had been reached, film makers could explore possibilities
    • camera can move closer or father
    • camera could move
    • faster editing
  • feature length films (1916, 1917)

Maturity

  • Content of film becomes married to the medium
  • silent film can be seen as a separate entity from sound film
    • early sound film not as good as late silent film
    • changed the nature of film, and the kinds of performers you need
  • the advent of television overturns the institutions of the old studio era
    • movies change their character again
  • go from Fred Ott's Sneeze to Chaplin's Modern Times

Film Permeates American Life

  • film as an "embedded social form"
  • deeply infiltrated into the personal minds
